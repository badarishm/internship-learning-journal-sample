#  Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is the process of examining datasets to understand their structure, patterns, and relationships before applying advanced analytical methods. The goal of EDA is to summarize the main characteristics of data and identify any issues such as missing values, outliers, or inconsistencies.

## Understanding the Dataset:
The first step in EDA is to understand the dataset by examining its size, structure, and types of variables. This includes identifying the number of rows and columns, understanding the meaning of each variable, and determining whether the data is numerical, categorical, or textual.

## Data Cleaning:
Before analysis, it is important to clean the data. This includes handling missing values, removing duplicate records, correcting inconsistent data formats, and ensuring that variables are stored in the correct data types. Clean data ensures more accurate analysis and reliable results.

## Descriptive Statistics:
EDA involves calculating descriptive statistics such as mean, median, standard deviation, minimum, and maximum values. These statistics help summarize the overall distribution and central tendencies of the data.

## Data Visualization:
Visualization plays an important role in EDA. Charts such as histograms, scatter plots, bar charts, and box plots help analysts understand distributions, relationships, and anomalies in the dataset. Visual analysis often reveals insights that are difficult to detect through numerical summaries alone.

## Identifying Patterns and Relationships:
EDA also helps identify correlations and relationships between variables. By analyzing how variables interact, analysts can discover trends, dependencies, and potential predictive factors.

## Detecting Outliers:
Outliers are unusual data points that differ significantly from the rest of the dataset. Detecting and understanding outliers is important because they can either indicate data errors or highlight meaningful rare events.

## Importance of EDA:
Exploratory Data Analysis is a crucial step in the data analysis workflow because it helps analysts better understand their data before building predictive models or performing advanced statistical analysis.
